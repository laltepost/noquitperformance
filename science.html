01

What is
phenylcapsaicin?

Phenylcapsaicin is a synthetic capsaicinoid — a structural analog of capsaicin, the compound found in chili peppers responsible for their heat.

Unlike natural capsaicin, phenylcapsaicin has been engineered for improved bioavailability and a cleaner sensory profile — meaning more active compound reaches the relevant receptors, with less of the gastric irritation associated with high doses of natural capsaicin.

The compound acts primarily as a TRPV1 agonist, triggering cellular responses that have been observed to support energy metabolism and exercise performance in multiple published studies.

aXivite® is a standardized, patented form of phenylcapsaicin used in the research we reference. NØ QUIT uses aXivite® at the clinically-studied dose.

Compound Class

Capsaicinoid

02

What the research observed.

Published research on phenylcapsaicin (aXivite®) has observed effects on several key exercise performance markers. Here's what the data shows.

Observed Effects

Performance & Metabolism

  • Improvements in VO₂ max (maximal oxygen uptake) in trained athletes
  • Increased time-to-exhaustion during high-intensity exercise protocols
  • Enhanced fat oxidation during aerobic and high-intensity exercise
  • Improved metabolic efficiency — more output per unit of energy

Observed Effects

Recovery & Readiness

  • Faster restoration of performance markers between sessions
  • Reduced perception of effort at equivalent work intensities
  • Improved training readiness indicators in repeat-effort protocols
  • Effects observed at the 2.5mg daily dose range

Research Context

The studies referenced used trained athletes in controlled laboratory settings. Individual results vary. Research findings describe observed effects under study conditions and do not constitute a guarantee of equivalent individual outcomes. NØ QUIT makes no claims beyond the structure/function language supported by the available research.

What it means.
What it doesn't.

What the science supports

Phenylcapsaicin supports energy metabolism during high-intensity exercise. The compound appears to improve how the body generates and uses energy at peak effort.

Research observes improvements in time-to-exhaustion. This suggests the compound may support the ability to sustain intensity for longer before fatigue forces a reduction in effort.

Recovery-related markers improved between sessions in studied protocols. Consistent daily use — not just pre-workout use — appears to be the effective approach.

What the science does not claim

This is not a fat-loss product. While fat oxidation was observed during exercise, NØ QUIT makes no body composition claims. The research context is exercise performance.

No disease treatment claims. This product is a dietary supplement. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease or medical condition.

No guaranteed performance outcomes. Research describes statistical effects in study populations. Individual responses vary. NØ QUIT is a support tool — training, sleep, and nutrition remain foundational.

04

The protocol.

01
Daily Dose

Take 2 capsules daily (2.5mg total — 1.25mg per capsule). Research effects were observed at consistent daily use — not single-dose acute loading. Do not exceed 2 capsules per day.

02
On Training Days

Take 30–60 minutes before your training session for alignment with the window used in exercise studies. Take with water.

03
On Rest Days

Take with your first meal of the day. Consistent daily use — regardless of training — appears to support sustained effects across the week.

Note: If you are pregnant, nursing, taking medications, or have a medical condition, consult your healthcare provider before use. Keep out of reach of children. Store in a cool, dry place below 77°F (25°C).
